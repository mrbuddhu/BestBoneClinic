Arthroscopy Surgery Advanced

Minimally Invasive Joint Treatment

Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive procedure that allows accurate diagnosis and treatment of joint conditions. Dr. Bipin Solanki, a leading orthopedic surgeon in Delhi, specializes in arthroscopic surgery to help patients restore mobility and live pain-free.

What is Arthroscopy Surgery?

Arthroscopy is a surgical procedure used to visualize, diagnose, and treat problems inside a joint. A small camera, called an arthroscope, is inserted through tiny incisions, providing a clear view of the joint's interior. This technique allows for accurate treatment with minimal trauma to surrounding tissues.

Conditions Treated with Arthroscopy

  • Knee Injuries: Meniscus tears, ACL reconstruction, cartilage damage
  • Shoulder Injuries: Rotator cuff tears, impingement syndrome, labral tears
  • Hip Issues: Femoroacetabular impingement (FAI), hip labral tears
  • Elbow & Wrist Problems: Tennis elbow, ligament injuries
  • Ankle Conditions: Ankle instability, cartilage damage

Recovery & Rehabilitation

After arthroscopy, patients may have mild swelling and discomfort, which typically improves within a few days. Physical therapy helps restore joint strength and mobility. Most patients return to daily activities within a few weeks, with full recovery depending on the condition treated.

What to Expect During the Procedure

Arthroscopy surgery is performed under anesthesia, where Dr. Bipin Solanki makes small incisions around the joint and inserts an arthroscope. Using specialized instruments, he repairs or removes damaged tissues. The procedure typically takes 30 to 90 minutes, depending on the complexity.
